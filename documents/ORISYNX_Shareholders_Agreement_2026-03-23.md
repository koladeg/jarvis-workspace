## Key Terms

### Share Capital (Clause 4)
- **Current:** Equal 1/3 shareholding (33.33% each for 3 shareholders)
- **Upon 4th Admission:** Equal 25% each (4 shareholders)
- **Structure:** Equal shareholding is fundamental

### Board & Management (Clause 5)
- All shareholders are directors
- Board meetings: minimum quarterly
- Decisions: simple majority (except Reserved Matters)
- Quorum: all directors present

### Reserved Matters (Clause 6) - Require Unanimous Consent
- M&A, Articles changes
- New shares/classes
- Disposal, winding up, liquidation
- Assets >NGN 500,000
- Mergers, reconstructions
- New shareholder admission
- Borrowing >NGN 1,000,000
- Auditor appointment
- Annual budget & business plan
- Principal business changes
- Dividend declaration
- Related-party transactions
- Material litigation

### Fourth Shareholder Admission (Clause 7)
- Requires unanimous approval
- Must execute Deed of Accession (Schedule 2)
- Becomes equal 25% shareholder
- Appointed as director
- No existing shareholder dilution—fresh allotment

### Founder Share Vesting (Clause 21) - **CRITICAL**

| Milestone | Vesting Date | Cumulative % | Conditions |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|-----------|

### Forfeiture Events (Bad Leaver - 21.6)
Automatic loss of unvested shares if:
- Voluntary resignation/withdrawal
- Removal for cause (gross misconduct, fraud, neglect)
- Failure to meet vesting conditions (+ 30-day cure period)
- Abandonment of duties (90+ days without consent)
- Insolvency/bankruptcy
- Criminal conviction (dishonesty/fraud/reputational damage)
- Breach of non-compete (Clause 14)

**Consequence:** All unvested shares forfeited at **nil consideration** (₦0.00)  
**Redistribution:** Forfeited shares go to remaining founders equally

### Good Leaver Exception (21.8)
- **Death:** Legal representatives inherit all shares (vested + unvested)
- **Permanent total disability:** Immediate full vesting of all shares
- No forfeiture applies

### Vesting Acceleration (21.11)
All unvested shares vest immediately upon:
- Full acquisition by third party (via Drag-Along)
- Sale of all/substantially all assets
- Dissolution by unanimous resolution

### Transfer & Pre-emption (Clause 9)
- No transfer without unanimous consent (except as provided in agreement)
- 30-day pre-emption right for remaining shareholders at offered price
- If not exercised, 90-day window to sell to third party
- No pledge/charge of shares without consent
- Violations are null and void

### Drag-Along Rights (Clause 11)
- Shareholders holding 75%+ can force minority to sell on same terms
- 30 Business Days' notice required

### Tag-Along Rights (Clause 12)
- Minority can require inclusion if majority selling 25%+ shares
- 30 Business Days' notice, 15 Business Days to elect

### Exit Provisions (Clause 17)
- 60 Business Days' notice to exit
- Remaining shareholders have 30-day right of first refusal at Fair Market Value
- If not purchased, company may buy and cancel (if permissible under law)
- Otherwise, pre-emption rights apply (Clause 9)

### Dispute Resolution (Clause 18)
1. Negotiation (21 Business Days)
2. Mediation (Lagos Multi-Door Courthouse - LMDC)
3. Arbitration in Lagos (if mediation fails in 30 days)
   - Sole arbitrator per Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023
   - English language
   - Final & binding

### Non-Compete (Clause 14)
- 2 years post-exit
- No competing business without consent
- No employee solicitation without consent

### Confidentiality (Clause 13)
- 5-year post-termination survival
- Exceptions: legal/regulatory requirement, professional advisers, public domain

### Dividends (Clause 8)
- Proportional to shareholding
- Requires unanimous approval
- No guarantee
