# GMAT Study Plan

Target score: **700+**  
Study window: **8 weeks**  
Recommended exam date: **Early May 2026**

---

## 1) Study Principles

- Study **6 days per week**, 2–3 hours on weekdays, 4–5 hours on one weekend day.
- Take **1 full-length diagnostic immediately**.
- Focus hardest on **Data Insights / Quant fundamentals / Critical Reasoning**.
- Keep an **error log** for every missed question.
- If mock scores are below 650 after Week 5, book a backup retake.

## 4) 8-Week Plan

## Week 1 — Baseline + Foundations
**Goal:** Understand current level and rebuild core habits.

- Take diagnostic mock
- Review every wrong answer
- Start arithmetic, algebra, word problems refresh
- Start sentence meaning / critical reasoning basics
- Set up error log with columns: topic, mistake type, reason, fix

**Target output:**
- 1 diagnostic completed
- 50–80 focused practice questions
- Weakness list finalized

## Week 2 — Quant Core + Critical Reasoning
**Goal:** Clean up fundamentals.

- Quant: ratios, percentages, equations, inequalities, number properties
- Verbal: argument structure, assumptions, strengthen/weaken
- Data Insights: chart/table interpretation
- 1 timed mixed quiz midweek

**Target output:**
- 80–100 questions
- Accuracy target: 65%+

## Week 3 — Problem Solving + Data Insights
**Goal:** Improve execution and timing.

- Quant: geometry, algebra translation, multi-step problems
- Data Insights: multi-source reasoning, two-part analysis
- Verbal: inference and logic traps
- Review pacing errors, not just content errors

**Target output:**
- 90–110 questions
- First signs of timing control

## Week 4 — First Full Mock + Deep Review
**Goal:** Measure improvement.

- Sit full mock under strict exam conditions
- Spend 1–2 days reviewing the mock deeply
- Drill weakest topic clusters only
- Practice educated guessing strategy

**Checkpoint target:**
- Aim to be trending toward **640–680+** by now

## Week 5 — Advanced Practice
**Goal:** Push toward 700 range.

- Hard quant sets
- Hard critical reasoning sets
- Mixed section sets under time pressure
- 1 essay / application task hour replaced by light study only if overloaded

**Target output:**
- 100+ questions
- Accuracy target on strong topics: 75%+

## Week 6 — Second Full Mock + Weakness Repair
**Goal:** Decide if target is realistic on first attempt.

- Take full mock
- Compare score trajectory
- Drill only recurring misses
- Practice section order and stamina

**Checkpoint decision:**
- If below 650: consider backup date
- If 660–690: stay aggressive
- If 700+: preserve and sharpen

## Week 7 — Exam Mode
**Goal:** Convert preparation into score.

- 2 timed section drills
- 1 full mock or half-length adaptive drill
- Light review of formulas/patterns
- Sleep and routine discipline

**Focus:**
- Timing
- Calm decision-making
- No panic on hard questions

## Week 8 — Taper Week
**Goal:** Arrive fresh, not burned out.

- Light practice only
- Review error log and favorite shortcuts
- Revisit top 25 recurring mistakes
- Prepare exam-day logistics

**48 hours before exam:**
- No cramming
- Sleep well
- Confirm ID, route, timing, scratch materials rules

## 6) Score Improvement Rules

### To reach 700+
- Prioritize **accuracy before speed** for 3 weeks, then tighten pacing.
- Track **why** you miss questions:
  - content gap
  - careless error
  - timing pressure
  - misread question
  - trap answer choice
- Re-do missed questions after 48 hours.
- Keep a list of “never again” errors.

---

## 8) If You Need a Retake

Retake quickly if:
- score is below 680,
- performance dropped due to nerves,
- mock history suggests higher ability.

Do **not** retake immediately without a revised plan. Use the first score report to target your exact weak areas.
