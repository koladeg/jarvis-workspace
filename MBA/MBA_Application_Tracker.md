## 2) Decision Lens: Canada vs Australia

| Factor | Canada | Australia |
|---|---|---|
| Post-study work permit | Often up to **3 years** for eligible multi-year programs | Often around **2 years** for eligible graduates |
| Distance to USA | **Closer**; easier networking/relocation | **Farther**; USA relocation is a bigger jump |
| Startup hubs | Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal | Sydney, Melbourne |
| Long-term USA startup path | Stronger geographic bridge | Still possible, but less direct |
| Climate / lifestyle | Colder, North American market exposure | Warmer, Asia-Pacific market exposure |
| Best fit if end goal is USA startup | **Stronger default path** | Good alternative if school fit/funding is better |

### Working recommendation
If the long-term goal is **launching a startup in the USA**, the **Canada path is slightly more strategic** because of:
- easier North America networking,
- better proximity for cross-border hiring/investors,
- a simpler narrative from MBA → work permit → US market entry.

Australia remains a solid option if:
- scholarship or tuition value is clearly better,
- admissions odds are stronger,
- the school’s entrepreneurship ecosystem fits better.

## 7) Visa / Work Permit Comparison

| Topic | Canada | Australia |
|---|---|---|
| Student visa route | Study permit | Student visa (subclass 500) |
| Post-study work | Often PGWP pathway; length depends on eligibility/program | Temporary Graduate visa pathway; duration depends on current rules and qualification |
| Typical advantage | Strong North America positioning | Strong Asia-Pacific positioning |
| For startup ambition | Easier bridge to US ecosystem | Good regional ecosystem but farther from US |
| Key caution | Program/school eligibility matters | Visa rules change often; verify current subclass conditions |

## 8) Essay Prompts Preview

These themes come up repeatedly across MBA applications:
- **Why MBA, and why now?**
- **Why this school?**
- **What are your short-term and long-term career goals?**
- **Describe a leadership experience.**
- **Describe a setback, difficult decision, or failure.**
- **How will you contribute to the class/community?**
- **Optional essay:** low GPA, gaps, weak quant profile, employment transition

### Example: McGill essay prompts already published
1. Why MBA now, and what are your mid-term / long-term post-MBA goals? How will McGill help?  
2. What qualities do you value in an MBA teammate, and how will your background enhance others’ experience?  
3. Describe a difficult decision and your decision-making process.
