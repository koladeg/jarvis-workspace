# AdugboInsure Grant Application Template

**Generated from 3-month pilot proposal (March 2024)**

## 1. MISSION & PROBLEM STATEMENT

**Mission (1-2 sentences):**
AdugboInsure is a digital community health insurance (CHI) solution designed to accelerate and expand the rollout of Community Health Insurance across Oyo State by supporting the Oyo State Health Insurance Agency (OYSHIA). We bridge awareness, enrollment, and trust gaps around CHI schemes through a tech-enabled, agent-driven model.

**Problem Statement:**
While OYSHIA successfully piloted CHI in five rural communities, urban adoption remains minimal. Low awareness, affordability barriers (₦13,500 annual premium), and lack of last-mile enrollment infrastructure prevent urban community members—especially informal earners with irregular income—from accessing basic health insurance.

**Key Insight:**
POS agents (widespread in Nigeria, handle ₦18 trillion/year in transactions) are the missing distribution channel. They're community-trusted, financially literate, and positioned to enroll and collect CHI payments.

## 9. QUICK-FILL ANSWERS FOR COMMON GRANT QUESTIONS

**"What problem do you solve?"**
> We make community health insurance accessible and affordable for Nigeria's informal economy through a POS agent-driven digital platform.

**"Who is your customer?"**
> Community members earning daily/weekly wages in Oyo State urban areas; enabling health insurance at ₦3,375/quarter.

**"What is your business model?"**
> Transaction-based: We earn ₦500 per enrollee from payment processing; partner with government (no subscription).

**"How do you measure impact?"**
> Monthly enrollments, churn rate, premium collection %, PHC service utilization (OPD visits, delivery rate), cost per acquisition.

**"Why now?"**
> Post-2023 cash scarcity drove POS adoption (₦18T/year), creating a ready distribution channel. Urban CHI rollout by Oyo State creates policy tailwind.

**"What's your unfair advantage?"**
> Direct partnership with OYSHIA + deep understanding of POS economics + trust with informal community members.
