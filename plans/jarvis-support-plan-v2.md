### 3A. ADUGBOINSURE (Priority 1 — 14 hrs/week)

**Current state:** 9 active agents in Apete, 6 recruited (inactive) in Abe Emu. Needs ~$5,000+ for expansion. Premiums too high.

#### Jarvis's Responsibilities

**Funding Radar (Every 3-4 days)**
- Scan: African impact investment funds, insurtech grants, DFI programs (IFC, AfDB), Nigerian government SME grants (BOI, NIRSAL), startup competitions
- Sources to monitor:
  - VC4A, Briter Bridges, Disrupt Africa (funding announcements)
  - NAICOM regulatory updates
  - Lagos State innovation grants
  - International: Mastercard Foundation, Gates Foundation health/finance intersections
  - Y Combinator, Techstars, Google for Startups Africa
- Output: Short brief — opportunity name, amount, deadline, fit score (1-5), application link
- Handoff: Kolade reviews, picks targets, Jarvis drafts applications

**POS Agent Educational Content (Weekly — Monday)**
- Format: Simple visual guides or scripts suitable for NotebookLM video generation
- Topics rotation:
  - Week 1: Product knowledge (what each insurance plan covers)
  - Week 2: Sales techniques (how to pitch micro-insurance)
  - Week 3: Claims process (what to tell customers)
  - Week 4: Objection handling (common pushbacks)
- Language: Simple English or Yoruba-friendly phrasing
- Handoff: Kolade reviews, distributes to agents via WhatsApp

**Agent Activation Plan (Abe Emu — 6 agents)**
- Jarvis drafts: Onboarding checklist, activation timeline, training materials
- Key questions for Kolade to decide:
  - What blocked activation? (funding? training? logistics?)
  - Minimum viable activation — can 2-3 start before all 6?
  - What does each agent need? (materials, ID cards, product sheets)
- Jarvis tracks: Activation milestones per agent

**Cost Optimization Research (Bi-weekly)**
- Research: Reinsurance options, group negotiation with underwriters, parametric insurance models, pay-as-you-go structures
- Benchmark: Compare AdugboInsure premium structure vs. competitors (Curacel, Casava, Hygeia)
- Output: Cost reduction memo with specific actionable recommendations

**Expansion Strategy (Monthly)**
- Location scoring: Population density, POS agent density, competitor presence, transport access
- Next-location shortlist with pros/cons
- Unit economics projection per new location

#### Success Metrics
- Funding applications submitted: ≥2/month
- Agent activation: 6 Abe Emu agents active within 6 weeks
- Premium cost: Identify ≥1 viable cost reduction path within 4 weeks
- Educational content: 4 pieces/month delivered

## 7. POTENTIAL CHALLENGES & MITIGATIONS

### Challenge 1: Time Overload
**Risk:** 9 projects competing for ~50 waking hours/week. Burnout is real.
**Mitigation:**
- Jarvis enforces priority tiers. AdugboInsure > Orisynx > SiFax always.
- Lower-priority items (truck, investments, programming) are "Jarvis-heavy" — Jarvis does 80% of work, Kolade reviews.
- Weekly review explicitly asks: "What should we pause this week?"
- If Kolade misses 2+ weeks on a lower-priority project, Jarvis flags it for conscious parking or delegation.

### Challenge 2: Funding Rejection
**Risk:** Grant/investment applications fail. AdugboInsure stalls.
**Mitigation:**
- Diversify: Apply to multiple funding sources simultaneously
- Bootstrap path: Jarvis researches revenue-first growth options (agent commission models, partnerships with existing insurers)
- Crowdfunding backup: Research GoFundMe/Kickstarter viability for social impact angle

### Challenge 3: Context Switching Tax
**Risk:** Jumping between 9 projects destroys deep work.
**Mitigation:**
- Batch similar work (all research on one day, all admin on another)
- Jarvis delivers updates at predictable times — no random interruptions
- Each project has a "decision queue" — Kolade reviews decisions in one sitting, not scattered throughout the day

### Challenge 4: Jarvis Token/Cost Budget
**Risk:** Supporting 9 projects burns through API budget fast.
**Mitigation:**
- Use cheapest models for routine tasks (Haiku for scans, summaries)
- Batch web searches (one search session per topic, not fragmented)
- Template-based outputs where possible
- Track daily spend; throttle lower-priority research if budget is tight

### Challenge 5: Stale Information
**Risk:** Jarvis works on outdated context (funding closed, job filled, truck sold).
**Mitigation:**
- Morning briefing includes "assumptions check" — quick flags for Kolade to confirm/correct
- Weekly review syncs all project statuses
- Trackers have "last verified" dates

### Challenge 6: Visa Timeline Pressure
**Risk:** Canada immigration has hard deadlines and long processing times.
**Mitigation:**
- Jarvis creates reverse-timeline from target date (end 2026)
- Critical path items flagged early (IELTS booking takes months, ECA takes weeks)
- Bi-weekly check ensures nothing slips
